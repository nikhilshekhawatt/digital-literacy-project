I created my digital literacy poster using Canva, an easy-to-use online design tool that allows users to create professional graphics with templates, icons, and text styling. The design highlights the importance of digital literacy for students in a clear and visually engaging manner.

My poster explains key aspects of digital literacy, including safe internet practices, the importance of using strong passwords, and avoiding suspicious links. It also emphasizes maintaining a professional online presence through platforms like LinkedIn and GitHub. Additionally, it covers email etiquette, such as using proper subject lines and maintaining a formal tone, and raises awareness about cybercrime by advising users not to share OTPs or UPI PINs. I also included useful digital tools like Canva, Google Forms, GitHub, and HackerRank to show practical applications.

One thing I found interesting while making this design was how different colors, layouts, and fonts can significantly improve readability and impact. However, balancing the content and layout without making the poster look crowded was slightly challenging. Overall, this task helped me understand both digital awareness and basic design principles.
