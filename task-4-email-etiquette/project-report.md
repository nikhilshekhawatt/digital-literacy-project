One situation where poor digital communication caused a problem could be a student sending an incomplete or unclear email to a professor about an assignment submission. For example, the student might write a vague message without a proper subject line, greeting, or clear explanation. As a result, the professor may misunderstand the request or ignore the email, leading to missed deadlines or confusion.

This issue could have been avoided by following proper email etiquette. The student should have included a clear subject line, used a polite greeting, and explained the situation briefly and clearly. Providing all necessary details, such as the assignment name and reason for the request, would have improved communication.

Effective digital communication ensures clarity, avoids misunderstandings, and helps maintain professionalism in academic and professional environments.
